# Story — The Format Grandparents Are Made Of

## Core Concept
Every grandparent is a walking archive.
The problem is no one asked them to open it.
The "Talking Memoir" platform exists to open that archive — one story at a time.

## PHL Frame
```
INPUT:  "Tell me about a time when..."
FILTER: Conflict → Choice → Consequence → Reflection
OUTPUT: A 3-minute story with a lesson at the end
```

## Editorial Prompts

**Story extraction:**
> "Tell me about a time when everything went wrong — and what you did next."

**Vivid detail:**
> "What did it smell like? Sound like? Who else was in the room?"

**Legacy question:**
> "What do you want people to remember about this story?"

## Story Structure (for video scripts)
```
Hook     (0:00–0:15)  — The moment: "I was 34, standing in the rain with nothing."
Context  (0:15–0:45)  — Who, where, when — just enough
Conflict (0:45–1:30)  — The real problem — what nobody knew
Turn     (1:30–2:00)  — The moment everything changed
Lesson   (2:00–2:30)  — What it actually means
Call     (2:30–3:00)  — What you want the listener to do with it
```
