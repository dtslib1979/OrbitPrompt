# Wisdom — The Currency Grandparents Have

## Core Concept
Wisdom is not knowledge. Knowledge can be searched.
Wisdom is what you carry after the search fails.
Grandparents have it because they ran out of options and survived anyway.

## PHL Frame
```
INPUT:  A grandparent's decision (any culture, any context)
FILTER: What did they know that no book told them?
OUTPUT: The principle behind the decision — one line.
```

## Editorial Prompts

**Opening question:**
> "What's the best advice you ever ignored — and then had to learn the hard way?"

**Depth question:**
> "What do you know now that would have saved you years of struggle?"

**Closing question:**
> "What do you want the next generation to stop doing?"

## Key Concepts
- Earned wisdom: can only come from lived experience, not study
- Contrast wisdom: knowing what NOT to do is often more valuable
- Quiet wisdom: grandparents rarely announce it — you have to ask
- Transferable wisdom: the specific story carries the universal lesson
