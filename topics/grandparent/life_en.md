# Life — What Grandparents Know

## Core Concept
Life is not a problem to be solved. It is a story to be told.
Grandparents carry the proof: they have lived through it.

## PHL Frame
```
INPUT:  A grandparent's life story (any language, any culture)
FILTER: What did they choose? What did they lose? What did they keep?
OUTPUT: The moment that changed everything — in one sentence.
```

## Editorial Prompts

**Opening question:**
> "Tell me one moment you wish you could give to someone younger."

**Depth question:**
> "What did you know at 70 that you didn't know at 30?"

**Closing question:**
> "If your life were a book, what would the title be?"

## Key Concepts (for content generation)
- Time: life is not a line but a spiral — returning with more understanding
- Loss: what we lost shapes us as much as what we gained
- Continuity: grandparents are the living link between past and future
- Simplicity: the older the wisdom, the simpler the words
